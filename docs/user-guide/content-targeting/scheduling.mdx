# Scheduling

Scheduling allows you to control when content becomes visible and when it expires. This is essential for time-sensitive campaigns, seasonal content, event promotions, and regulatory announcements that must go live or be removed at specific times.

## Scheduling Fields

Two fields control content scheduling. Both are available on pages, services, news articles, and products in the sidebar panel of the content editor.

| Field | Type | Purpose |
|-------|------|---------|
| **scheduledPublishAt** | Day and Time picker | Content becomes visible **after** this date and time |
| **expiresAt** | Day and Time picker | Content is **hidden after** this date and time |

### scheduledPublishAt

When set, the content will not appear on the frontend until the specified date and time, even if the content has `published` status. This enables you to prepare and publish content in advance, with it automatically becoming visible at the scheduled time.

- **Not set:** Content is visible immediately upon publishing (subject to other filters).
- **Set to a future date:** Content is hidden until that date and time arrives.
- **Set to a past date:** Content is visible (the scheduled date has already passed).

### expiresAt

When set, the content will automatically stop appearing on the frontend after the specified date and time. The content is not deleted -- it remains in the CMS and can be reactivated by clearing or extending the expiry date.

- **Not set:** Content never expires and remains visible indefinitely.
- **Set to a future date:** Content will be hidden when that date and time arrives.
- **Set to a past date:** Content is already expired and hidden from the frontend.

## Using Both Fields Together

You can use `scheduledPublishAt` and `expiresAt` together to create a precise visibility window:

| scheduledPublishAt | expiresAt | Result |
|--------------------|-----------|--------|
| Not set | Not set | Always visible (when published) |
| 1 March 2026 09:00 | Not set | Visible from 1 March 2026 onwards |
| Not set | 31 March 2026 23:59 | Visible until 31 March 2026 |
| 1 March 2026 09:00 | 31 March 2026 23:59 | Visible only during March 2026 |

## How Scheduling Combines with Content Filters

Scheduling is one of three content targeting mechanisms. All three are combined with AND logic when filtering content on the frontend:

1. **Jurisdiction targeting** -- Is this content available for the visitor's jurisdiction?
2. **Language targeting** -- Is this content available in the visitor's language?
3. **Scheduling** -- Is the current date/time within the content's visibility window?

Content must pass all three filters to appear. For example, a page targeted to the United Kingdom, in English only, with `scheduledPublishAt` of 1 March 2026 will only appear on the UK English site after 1 March 2026.

## Performance

Both `scheduledPublishAt` and `expiresAt` fields are **indexed** in the database for performance. This means scheduling filters do not add significant query overhead, even across the full content set of 250+ jurisdictions.

## Common Use Cases

### Campaign Landing Pages

Set `scheduledPublishAt` to the campaign start date and `expiresAt` to the campaign end date. The page automatically appears and disappears without manual intervention.

### Seasonal Content

Create holiday or seasonal content well in advance, then schedule it to appear during the appropriate period (e.g., a year-end promotion visible only in December).

### Regulatory Announcements

Set `scheduledPublishAt` to the exact date and time a regulation takes effect. The content will not appear prematurely.

### Event Promotions

Schedule event-related content to expire after the event has concluded, keeping the site current without manual cleanup.

## Tips

- Always verify the timezone used by the date/time picker. Scheduled times are evaluated against the server's timezone.
- Scheduling does not override status. Content must have `published` status **and** be within the scheduling window to appear on the frontend.
- Use the [Analytics Overview](/docs/user-guide/analytics) dashboard to see a list of upcoming scheduled content.
- To remove scheduling constraints, simply clear the date/time fields and save.
